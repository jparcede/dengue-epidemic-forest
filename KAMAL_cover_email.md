# Cover email draft — to Kamal, Nuning, and Maria

**Subject:** A few decisions on the dengue epidemic-forest paper (SoL, headline finding, scope)

Hi Kamal, Nuning, and Maria,

Before we take the South Cotabato dengue epidemic-forest paper much further, I wanted
to loop you all in on a few things while they're still easy to adjust — starting with
how we position it relative to the Nasution 2024 Jakarta paper, since Nuning and Kamal
are on both.

The short version is that I've written the paper to attribute the region-scale and
case-prevalence ideas squarely to Nasution 2024, and to frame our contribution as the
connectivity follow-up it points to — replacing the fixed adjacency with a
probabilistic distance-decay network and reconstructing the forest as a Monte-Carlo
ensemble with per-edge stability (plus it being the first Philippine dengue
application). In other words, we build the "inter-regional connectivity and networks"
piece your Discussion flags as future work.

Two things I'd especially like your read on. First, I've now **harmonised our
Strength of Linkage to your exact Eq. 5**, so both papers use one identical linkage
definition — cleaner, and it makes our contribution unambiguously the connectivity
+ ensemble layer. Second, and more importantly: doing that **changed a central
result.** Global R_t and General Santos City staying terminal are unchanged, but the
dominant source flips from rural Surallah to **Koronadal City, the provincial
capital.** The attached note lays out exactly what moved and what didn't.

One reassuring point: the flipped finding is independently corroborated — a 2025
scan-statistic study of South Cotabato dengue found the dominant cluster over
Polomolok and Koronadal City, so our Koronadal-as-source result lines up with other
evidence rather than being a modelling artefact. I've also framed the paper as
methodological (quantified linkage uncertainty), demonstrated on this case.

Third, on scope: I applied the identical pipeline to three more Region 12 provinces
(North Cotabato, Sultan Kudarat, Sarangani), and the same pattern recurs — the inland
provincial capital / population hub is the dominant source in each. I've reframed the
paper as *"a South Cotabato case study with preliminary cross-province validation in
Region 12,"* which lifts this into the title and abstract. It's clearly marked
preliminary (the coordinates and two of the populations still need authoritative
sources), so the open question for us is whether we're comfortable putting it in the
title now or keep it as a subsection until it's final. I've also added a short
**limitations** section covering the things we ran into (weak individual edges,
aggregation, model dependence, surveillance under-reporting, onset-reproducibility).

Could you take a look and tell me whether the harmonised SoL, the resulting change in
the headline finding, and the scope/title reframe sit right with you all? The old SoL
is still one toggle away if you'd rather keep it, so nothing is locked in — details
and the exact talking points are in the attached note.

Happy to hop on a call if that's easier.

Thanks,
Jayrold
